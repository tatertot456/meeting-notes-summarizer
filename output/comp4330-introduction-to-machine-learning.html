Why Machine Learning & Data Science?

▶

We live in an era of explosive data growth — from terabytes to petabytes to exabytes and beyond. Data is collected from everywhere: businesses, scientific instruments, social media, wearables, and more.

"We are drowning in data, but starving for knowledge." — Moore's Law era reality

Major sources of data include:

  • Business: Web, e-commerce, transactions, stocks
  • Science: Remote sensing, bioinformatics, scientific simulation
  • Society: News, digital cameras, social media, wearables

Moore's Law predicts 147 Zettabytes of data — the need to extract knowledge from this data is the core motivation for Machine Learning.

What Is Machine Learning & Artificial Intelligence?

▶

Machine Learning is the extraction of interesting, non-trivial, implicit, previously unknown, and potentially useful patterns or knowledge from huge amounts of data.

Machine Learning (KDD)

Knowledge Discovery in Databases — extracting patterns from data through automated analysis.

Artificial Intelligence

Perception of environment → Determination of recommended action based on extracted knowledge.

Data Science

Extraction of Actionable Knowledge from Big Data — characterized by Volume, Velocity, Variety, Veracity, and Value.

Alternative Names

KDD, knowledge extraction, data/pattern recognition, predictive analytics.

Evolution of Sciences

▶
  • Before 1600: Empirical science — observation-based
  • 1600–1950s: Theoretical science — mathematical models motivate experiments
  • 1950s–1990s: Computational science — simulation of complex models
  • 1990–now: Data science — flood of data from instruments, internet, and storage

AI represents the next step: taking action based on knowledge extracted from data, not just analyzing it.

What Kind of Data Can Be Mined?

▶

Machine Learning operates across a vast range of data types:

Relational & Warehouse

Databases, data warehouses, data lakes, transactional databases

Streams & Sensor

Real-time data streams, IoT sensor outputs, time-series data

Graph & Network

Social networks, multi-linked data, graph structures

Unstructured

Text, multimedia, web data, spatial and spatiotemporal data

What Kinds of Patterns Can Be Mined?

▶

Classification (Supervised)

Construct models based on training examples to predict class labels. Methods: decision trees, SVM, neural networks, logistic regression.

Cluster Analysis (Unsupervised)

Group data to form new categories. Maximizes intra-class similarity while minimizing interclass similarity.

Outlier Analysis

Detect data objects that don't comply with general behavior. Useful in fraud detection and rare event analysis.

Association Rules

Find interesting relationships between variables in large datasets. Classic example: market basket analysis.

Data Science vs. Data Mining

▶

Data Mining

A technique — the process of extracting useful information, patterns, and trends from huge databases to find actionable knowledge.

Data Science

A field — obtaining valuable insights from structured and unstructured data using various tools and methods, with a scientific approach emphasizing business growth and creative solutions.

Data Science is the confluence of: Machine Learning, Statistics, NLP, Algorithm Design, Pattern Recognition, HPC, Visualization, and Database Technology.
